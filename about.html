About VeloMax

Designed for performance. Driven by adventure. Discover our journey, our values, and the team guiding your next ride.

Our History

How VeloMax Began

VeloMax was founded in 2012 by three friends who shared a passion for road cycling climbs. What started as a local weekend cycling meetup soon expanded into a globally recognized adventure tour brand and coach network.

Over the last decade, we have mapped thousands of kilometers of gravel paths, Alpine cols, and canyon singletracks. Today, VeloMax supports thousands of active members, from children learning safety fundamentals to elite racers preparing for local criteriums.

2012

The First Spin

Started as a weekend training group of 15 members tackling local climbs. Core values of safety and community were born.

2016

Guided Expeditions Launch

Organized our first multi-day Alpine road tour. Created full logistics support, professional ride leading systems, and first-aid protocols.

2021

Coaching & MTB Integration

Opened our dedicated training academy. Added mountain bike trails and gravel challenges to meet growing off-road interest.

2026

Going Global

Now serving over 1,500 active members with 6 unique tour categories, premium rental fleets, and digital GPX planning tools.

What Drives Us

Our Core Philosophy

Every decision we make is guided by four core values that ensure high-quality rides and safety for all.

Safety First

First-aid certified ride leaders, emergency sag vehicles, and structured safety orientations are mandatory on every single ride.

Inclusive Growth

We support riders at every stage, from kids learning fundamentals to elite competitive racers developing their aerobic threshold.

Respect Outdoors

We adhere to Leave No Trace principles, help preserve local trail access, and support mountain trail stewardship programs.

Elite Quality

From carbon Specialized rentals to luxury pass hotels and certified UCI mentors, we never compromise on product quality.

Rigorous Standards

Uncompromising Safety Standards

Cycling in high alpine mountains and rugged forest descents requires professional logistics, clear communication, and preparedness.

Certified Ride Leaders
Every leader holds active Wilderness First Responder (WFR) and CPR certifications.
Active Communication & GPS Tracker
We utilize satellite tracking communicators and localized radio signals for instant connection.
Mechanics Support Vehicle
SAG vehicles trail our long tours carrying tires, replacement carbon parts, hydration, and medical gear.
